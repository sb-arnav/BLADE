## Why this shape

- **No "would love your support."** Cringes Miessler instantly per his own blog about cold pitches.
- **Opens with the build, not the pitch.** First sentence names what BLADE does technically.
- **Demo link in the second sentence, not the seventh.** He's a Twitter user; he'll watch a 75-second video before reading paragraph 4.
- **Frames PAI complementarity, not competition.** "Standalone-binary version of what PAI does inside Claude Code" is technically accurate AND gives him a reason to be positive.
- **"Genuinely don't expect anything"** is permission language. Lets him say no without guilt. Increases yes rate paradoxically.
- **Mentions Show HN timing** — he can opt to amplify or not without you asking.
- **Walk-you-through-the-architecture offer** — gives him a path to engage further that isn't "endorse my thing." Some founders take this, most don't; the offer is free.

## What NOT to include

- ~~Long backstory about why you built BLADE.~~ He doesn't care; he gets ~50 of these a week.
- ~~Roadmap, future features.~~ Ship signal, not promise signal.
- ~~Comparison tables.~~ The single sentence "standalone-binary version of PAI inside Claude Code" carries the whole map.
- ~~Asking him to install + test.~~ Big ask. The demo is the install-equivalent for first impression.
- ~~"Let me know what you think!"~~ Energy vampire phrase.
- ~~Pitch deck attachments.~~ Twitter DMs strip them; he'd see a broken icon.
