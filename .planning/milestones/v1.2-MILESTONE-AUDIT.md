## Executive Verdict

v1.2 shipped the chat-capability spine BLADE was wired for since v1.0. The substrate moved from "v1.1 substrate is reachable, configurable, and observable" to "v1.2 substrate can detect its own degradation (Doctor) and act on the world (chat → consent → cross-app write with ego refusal-elimination)."

What's *not* done is the operator-driven runtime layer — Phase 18's cold-install demo (JARVIS-12 e2e), Phase 17's Doctor pane runtime UI-polish UAT, and the entirety of Phase 19 (11 v1.1 UAT carry-overs). All three are operator-owned per the same convention used for v1.0 Mac smoke and v1.1 UAT carry-overs. Two are now constrained by the operator's API-key availability (cold-install demo, parts of Phase 19); one is explicitly deferred under the chat-first pivot (Doctor pane UAT).

**The pivot is the headline.** On 2026-04-30 mid-Phase-17 verification, the operator re-framed: "we only need a chat place capable enough to do anything, not a big box of beautiful UI with nothing functional inside." That changed v1.2's closure shape — UI-polish UAT was deferred for Phase 17, Phase 18 was reinterpreted text-chat-first (PTT/voice → v1.3), Phase 19 was deferred wholesale. Phase 18's chat → action loop IS the chat-first product direction, just without the voice surface yet.

No critical blockers. No unsatisfied code requirements. Tech debt is bounded, documented, and traceable.
